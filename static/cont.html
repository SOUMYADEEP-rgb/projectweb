Occurs when the Moon comes between the Sun and Earth, blocking the Sun's light partially or completely.

Types of Solar Eclipses:

  • Total Solar Eclipse: The Sun is completely covered by the Moon.
  • Partial Solar Eclipse: Only part of the Sun is covered.
  • Annular Solar Eclipse: The Moon covers the Sun's center, leaving a ring of light (annulus).

üåï Lunar Eclipse

Occurs when the Earth comes between the Sun and Moon, causing Earth's shadow to fall on the Moon.

Types of Lunar Eclipses:

  • Total Lunar Eclipse: The entire Moon is covered by Earth's shadow, turning it red (Blood Moon).
  • Partial Lunar Eclipse: Only a part of the Moon is covered.
  • Penumbral Lunar Eclipse: The Moon moves through Earth's penumbral (outer) shadow, causing a slight dimming.

Celestial bodies made of ice, dust, and rock that orbit the Sun in elongated paths.

When near the Sun, they develop a glowing coma and a tail due to solar radiation. The tail always points away from the Sun due to solar wind.

ü™® Asteroids

Small, rocky objects that orbit the Sun, mostly found in the Asteroid Belt between Mars and Jupiter.

Can be irregular in shape and vary in size from a few meters to hundreds of kilometers. Some asteroids come close to Earth and are called Near-Earth Objects (NEOs).

Example: Ceres (largest asteroid and a dwarf planet).
